# Sources & Further Reading

What this wiki was built from, roughly in order of how much weight it should carry.

---

## Primary

The things that are actually evidence.

**The films themselves** — *The Godfather* (1972), *Part II* (1974), *Part III* (1990), *The Godfather Coda* (2020), and the [recuts and restorations](../films/recuts-and-restorations.md). The 2008 Coppola Restoration and 2022 4K remasters are the versions to work from; earlier transfers misrepresent the [photography](../analysis/cinematography.md).

**The novels** — Mario Puzo, [*The Godfather*](../books/the-godfather-1969.md) (Putnam, 1969) and [*The Sicilian*](../books/the-sicilian-1984.md) (Random House, 1984); Mark Winegardner, [*The Godfather Returns*](../books/the-godfather-returns-2004.md) (2004) and [*The Godfather's Revenge*](../books/the-godfathers-revenge-2006.md) (2006); Ed Falco, [*The Family Corleone*](../books/the-family-corleone-2012.md) (2012).

**[*The Godfather Notebook*](../books/the-notebook.md)** — Francis Ford Coppola (Regan Arts, 2016). The facsimile of his working binder: Puzo's novel cut apart, pasted up, and annotated scene by scene with tone, intent, and pitfalls. **The single most valuable document about this film.**

**Coppola's DVD/Blu-ray commentaries** (recorded 2001, carried onto the 2008 and 2022 discs). Three full-length tracks. No substitute exists.

**"The Godfather Family: A Look Inside"** (1991) — Jeff Werner's 73-minute documentary on the disc supplements. Screen tests, rehearsal footage, and interviews with participants who are now dead.

**Contemporaneous interviews** — particularly anything recorded before about 1985, which predates the worst of the retrospective embellishment. See [Interviews](../media/interviews.md), especially the 1979 Coppola archival interview.

## Documented secondary

**Mark Seal, *Leave the Gun, Take the Cannoli: The Epic Story of the Making of The Godfather*** (Gallery, 2021). **The best account of the production.** Journalism rather than memoir, which is exactly what this subject needs — Seal cross-checks the participants against each other and says when he can't resolve a discrepancy.

**Peter Cowie, *The Godfather Book*** (Faber, 1997). The standard scholarly reference, and still the most rigorous treatment of the films as films.

**Harlan Lebo, *The Godfather Legacy*** (Simon & Schuster, 1997; updated). Production history, well documented.

**Jenny M. Jones, *The Annotated Godfather*** (Black Dog & Leventhal, 2007). Script-by-script annotation with production detail — extremely useful for scene-level checking.

**Peter Biskind, *Easy Riders, Raging Bulls*** (1998). The New Hollywood context. Enormously readable and to be treated with some caution — Biskind's sourcing is anecdotal and several subjects have disputed his accounts.

**Michael Sragow's National Film Registry essay** (2002) and the Library of Congress registry documentation.

---

## Memoirs — read with a hand on your wallet

**Robert Evans, *The Kid Stays in the Picture*** (1994). Irresistible, self-serving, and demonstrably unreliable in places. Evans claims a great deal of authorship that [Coppola](../people/francis-ford-coppola.md) has disputed for fifty years. Read it for the voice, not the facts.

**Al Pacino, *Sonny Boy*** (2024). Late-life memoir; good on the fear of being fired.

**Mario Puzo, *The Godfather Papers and Other Confessions*** (1972). Puzo on writing the book and the screenplay, published while it was all still fresh. Short and honest.

**Salvatore "Sonny" Grosso, Gianni Russo, and various participants' accounts.** Treat as claims rather than record. Several are uncorroborated and some are contradicted.

---

## On the real history

**Peter Maas, *The Valachi Papers*** (1968) — published a year before the novel, and one of Puzo's sources.

**Selwyn Raab, *Five Families*** (2005) — the standard history of the New York organization.

**Robert Lacey, *Little Man: Meyer Lansky and the Gangster Life*** (1991) — the [Hyman Roth](../characters/hyman-roth.md) material.

**T. J. English, *Havana Nocturne*** (2008) — the Cuban casino economy, and the best background for *Part II*'s Havana sequence.

**David Yallop, *In God's Name*** (1984) — the John Paul I conspiracy case. Influential, widely disputed, and clearly an input to [*Part III*](../films/the-godfather-part-iii-1990.md).

**Alexander Stille, *Excellent Cadavers*** (1995) — the Corleonesi, Falcone, and Borsellino. The corrective to any romance the films might leave you with.

**Francesco Rosi, *Salvatore Giuliano*** (1962, film) — on [the bandit](real-world-mafia.md#salvatore-giuliano), and better than Cimino's adaptation.

## On reliability

Three standing cautions that apply to almost everything on this list:

**The production anecdotes are contested.** Coppola, Evans, and Ruddy told incompatible versions of the same events for decades, each with an interest in the outcome. Where this wiki reports a disputed story, it names who claims it.

**The mob-adjacent stories are unverifiable and getting more so.** Lenny Montana's Colombo association is documented. Most of the rest rests on the testimony of people with reasons to embellish, and nearly all of them are now dead.

**Box office from the 1970s is a genre of fiction.** Rentals and gross get conflated, reissues get folded in and out, and the internet has been recirculating the same unsourced numbers for twenty years. See [Box Office](../data/box-office.md).
