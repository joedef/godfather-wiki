# Timeline

The Corleone story, 1901–1997, plus the real-world production history alongside it.

**Dates marked ≈ are inferred** from context rather than stated on screen. The films rarely date themselves, and the novel and [continuation novels](../continuity.md#tier-4-the-continuation-novels) sometimes disagree.

---

## In-universe

### The Sicilian origin

| Year | Event | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1891** ≈ | Vito Andolini born, Corleone, Sicily | *Part II* |
| **1901** | Antonio Andolini murdered by Don Ciccio. Paolo Andolini killed. Signora Andolini killed protecting Vito. | *Part II* |
| **1901** | Vito, aged 9, reaches Ellis Island. A clerk records his surname as **Corleone**. Quarantined for smallpox. | *Part II* |

### Building the family

| Year | Event | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1917** ≈ | Vito working in the Abbandando grocery; married to Carmela; **Sonny** born | *Part II* |
| **1917–18** | Don Fanucci forces Vito out of his job. Vito falls in with Clemenza and Tessio. | *Part II* |
| **1919** ≈ | **Fredo** born | *Part II* |
| **1920** | Vito kills **Don Fanucci** during the Feast of San Rocco | *Part II* |
| **1920** ≈ | **Michael** born | *Part II* |
| **1920–25** | Genco Pura Olive Oil founded with Genco Abbandando and Clemenza. Vito becomes the neighborhood's patron. | *Part II* |
| **1925** | Vito returns to Sicily and kills **Don Ciccio** | *Part II* |
| **1927** ≈ | **Connie** born | inferred |
| **1930s** | Vito consolidates power through Prohibition's end | *[The Family Corleone](../books/the-family-corleone-2012.md)* (1933–34) |
| **1933–34** | Sonny, aged 17, forces his way into the business. Tom Hagen groomed for consigliere. Luca Brasi recruited. | *The Family Corleone* |
| **1941** | **December 7.** Vito's birthday. Michael announces his Marine enlistment. The last time the family is intact. | *Part II* flashback |

### The war

| Year | Event | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1945** | **August.** Connie marries Carlo Rizzi. Vito hears petitions. Michael brings Kay. | *The Godfather* |
| **1945** | Genco Abbandando dies of cancer | *novel; [restored cuts](../films/deleted-scenes.md)* |
| **1945** | The Woltz affair — the horse's head | *The Godfather* |
| **1945** | Sollozzo's narcotics proposal refused. **Luca Brasi killed.** **Vito shot five times** and survives. | *The Godfather* |
| **1945** | Michael kills **Sollozzo and McCluskey**; flees to Sicily. Paulie Gatto killed. | *The Godfather* |
| **1946–48** | Michael in exile in Sicily | *The Godfather*; *[The Sicilian](../books/the-sicilian-1984.md)* dates its events to 1950 |
| **1948** | Michael marries **Apollonia Vitelli**. She is killed by a car bomb. | *The Godfather* |
| **1948** | **Sonny killed** at the Jones Beach Causeway toll plaza | *The Godfather* |
| **1948** | Vito convenes the Five Families and makes peace | *The Godfather* |

### Michael's ascent

| Year | Event | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1951** ≈ | Michael returns; marries **Kay Adams** | *The Godfather* |
| **1955** | **Anthony Corleone** born | inferred |
| **1955** | **Vito Corleone dies** in his garden | *The Godfather* |
| **1955** | **The baptism.** Barzini, Tattaglia, Cuneo, Stracci, and Moe Greene killed. Tessio executed. **Carlo Rizzi garroted.** | *The Godfather* |
| **1955** | Michael lies to Kay. The door closes. | *The Godfather* |
| **1955–58** | The family relocates to Nevada | *Part II* |
| **1955–62** | Winegardner's interquel — Nick Geraci, Francesca Corleone | *[The Godfather Returns](../books/the-godfather-returns-2004.md)* |
| **1958** | **Mary Corleone** born | inferred |

### The fall

| Year | Event | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1958** | Anthony's First Communion, Lake Tahoe. Senator Geary's bribe. Pentangeli's grievance. **Assassination attempt on Michael.** | *Part II* |
| **1958** | **December 31.** Havana. Michael learns Fredo betrayed him. Batista abdicates; the revolution takes the city. | *Part II* |
| **1959** | Senate committee hearings. **Pentangeli recants.** Kay reveals the abortion; Michael strikes her and banishes her. | *Part II* |
| **1959** | **Carmela Corleone dies.** | *Part II* |
| **1959** | **Hyman Roth killed** at Miami airport; Rocco Lampone killed. **Frank Pentangeli dies** in protective custody. **Fredo Corleone killed** on Lake Tahoe. | *Part II* |
| **1963–64** | Winegardner's sequel — Tom Hagen killed; the Shea assassination | *[The Godfather's Revenge](../books/the-godfathers-revenge-2006.md)* |
| **before 1979** | **Tom Hagen dies** — unexplained in the films | *Part III* |

### The end

| Year | Event | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1979** | Michael invested with the Order of St. Sebastian. **Vincent Mancini** joins the family. The Immobiliare deal proposed. | *Part III* |
| **1979** | **Joey Zasa's helicopter attack** on the Commission in Atlantic City. Michael's diabetic stroke. **Zasa killed** by Vincent. | *Part III* |
| **1979** | Michael's confession to **Cardinal Lamberto**, who is elected Pope John Paul I and dies 33 days later. | *Part III* |
| **1980** | Michael abdicates; **Vincent becomes Don**. The opera night: Keinszig, Altobello, Lucchesi, and Gilday killed. | *Part III* |
| **1980** | **Mary Corleone killed** on the steps of the Teatro Massimo | *Part III* |
| **1997** | **Michael Corleone dies alone** in a Sicilian courtyard | *Part III* — **removed in [Coda](../films/the-godfather-coda-2020.md)** |

## Real-world

| Date | Event |
| --- | --- |
| **1965** | Puzo publishes *The Fortunate Pilgrim*. It fails commercially. |
| **1967–68** | Puzo shops ~60 pages of *The Godfather*. **Paramount options film rights for $12,500 against $80,000.** |
| **March 10, 1969** | ***The Godfather* published** by G. P. Putnam's Sons |
| **1969–70** | 67 weeks on the bestseller list; 9M+ copies in two years |
| **April 14, 1970** | Puzo hired to adapt, $100,000 plus percentage |
| **August 10, 1970** | Puzo delivers first draft, ~150 pages |
| **September 28, 1970** | **Coppola announced as director** — $125,000 plus 6% of gross rentals |
| **February 1971** | [Ruddy](../people/albert-s-ruddy.md) reaches agreement with **Joe Colombo** and the Italian-American Civil Rights League |
| **March 29, 1971** | Shooting script delivered (163 pages); **principal photography begins** |
| **June 1971** | Colombo shot and permanently disabled at a League rally |
| **June 22, 1971** | Sonny's tollbooth sequence shot at Mitchel Field, Long Island |
| **August 6, 1971** | Principal photography ends |
| **March 14, 1972** | **World premiere**, Loew's State Theatre, New York |
| **March 24, 1972** | **US wide release** |
| **March 27, 1973** | 45th Academy Awards — 3 wins. **Brando declines Best Actor**; Sacheen Littlefeather appears in his place. |
| **October 1, 1973** | *Part II* begins shooting |
| **June 19, 1974** | *Part II* wraps |
| **December 12, 1974** | ***Part II* premiere**, Loew's Astor Plaza |
| **November 16 & 18, 1974** | NBC broadcasts *The Godfather* — a record $10M rights fee |
| **April 8, 1975** | 47th Academy Awards — ***Part II* wins 6**, the first sequel to win Best Picture |
| **November 18, 1977** | ***The Godfather Saga*** broadcast on NBC |
| **April 10, 1979** | **Nino Rota dies** |
| **1981** | *The Godfather Epic* VHS box set |
| **1988** | Coppola signs for *Part III* — $6M plus participation |
| **July 18, 1989** | Rebecca Schaeffer murdered, hours before her scheduled audition for Mary |
| **November 27, 1989** | *Part III* begins shooting |
| **December 25, 1990** | ***Part III* wide release** — $6M on Christmas Day, a record held until *Titanic* |
| **1992** | *The Godfather Trilogy: 1901–1980* released |
| **July 2, 1999** | **Mario Puzo dies**, ending the [*Part IV*](../films/unmade-part-iv.md) project |
| **October 9, 2001** | The Godfather DVD Collection |
| **July 1, 2004** | **Marlon Brando dies** |
| **March 21, 2006** | EA's *[The Godfather](../reference/video-games.md)* video game — Brando's last recorded performance |
| **September 23, 2008** | **The Coppola Restoration** released |
| **2012** | *[The Family Corleone](../books/the-family-corleone-2012.md)* published; Paramount and the Puzo estate sue each other |
| **2013** | The litigation settles |
| **May 18, 2014** | **Gordon Willis dies** |
| **November 2016** | *[The Godfather Notebook](../books/the-notebook.md)* published in facsimile |
| **October 26, 2019** | **Robert Evans dies** |
| **December 4, 2020** | ***The Godfather Coda*** released |
| **March 22, 2022** | **50th anniversary 4K restorations**; *[The Offer](../films/the-offer-2022.md)* premieres April 28 |
| **July 6, 2022** | **James Caan dies** |
| **May 25, 2024** | **Albert S. Ruddy dies** |
| **October 11, 2025** | **Diane Keaton dies** |
| **February 15, 2026** | **Robert Duvall dies** |
