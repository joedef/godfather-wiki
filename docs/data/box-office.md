# Box Office

<p class="epigraph">Any single number you see for The Godfather's gross is somebody's estimate.
</p>

---

## Why the first film's number is unrecoverable

Three reasons, and they compound:

**1. Studios reported *rentals*, not gross.** Through the 1970s, the trade press reported the distributor's share — typically 40–50% of ticket sales — rather than what audiences actually paid. Later citations routinely mix the two, which is how the same film appears as both $86 million and $250 million in reputable sources.

**2. It was reissued repeatedly.** 1972, 1973, 1997, 2022, plus international runs staggered over years. "Total gross" depends entirely on where you stop counting.

**3. Nobody was tracking carefully.** Systematic daily box office reporting didn't exist. Numbers came from studio press releases, which had incentives.

The commonly cited figure — **$250–291 million worldwide** — is a reconstruction across all releases. Treat it as a range, not a number.

## The 1972 records

Paramount took over **$15 million in advance rentals** before a frame was shown publicly. It opened in five theaters on March 15 and expanded to 316 within five days.

| Milestone | Figure |
| --- | --- |
| Opening day, 5 theaters | $57,829 |
| First week | $568,800 — #1 at the US box office |
| First five days, national | ~$6.8M |
| Second week | ~$10M — an industry record at the time |
| By April 9, 1972 | ~$26M |
| Weeks at #1 | 18+ |
| NBC broadcast rights (1974) | **$10 million** — then a record for a film |

New York theaters raised ticket prices from $3 to $4 and increased showings from four a day to seven. It finished as the **highest-grossing film released to that point**, before *Jaws* (1975) and *Star Wars* (1977) reset the scale entirely.

---

## Part III's Christmas record

It opened December 25, 1990 to **$19.6 million** across 1,901 screens, second behind *Home Alone*, and took **$6 million on Christmas Day** — then the biggest single day in December box office history. That record stood **seven years**, until *Titanic*.

## The other revenue

Theatrical is a fraction of what these films have earned:

- **NBC broadcast, 1974** — $10M, then a record. Ratings of 38.2 and 39.4 across the two nights, among the highest ever for a film on US television.
- **Home video, 1981 onward** — the 1981 *Epic* box set, the 1992 *Trilogy*, the 2001 DVD Collection, the [2008 Coppola Restoration](../films/recuts-and-restorations.md), the 2022 4K set. Paramount has sold this trilogy to the same customers approximately six times.
- **The [video games](../reference/video-games.md)** — EA's 2006 title sold over **4 million units** by 2008.
- **Perpetual licensing** — the Fujita cover design, the score, the imagery.
