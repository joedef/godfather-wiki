Two lines from the trilogy made *AFI's 100 Years…100 Movie Quotes* (2005):

| Rank | Line | Speaker | Film |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| **#2** | *"I'm gonna make him an offer he can't refuse."* | [Vito Corleone](../characters/vito-corleone.md) | *The Godfather* |

## The Godfather (1972)

> **"I believe in America. America has made my fortune."**
> — Amerigo Bonasera. The first words of the trilogy, spoken in total darkness by a man about to ask for two people to be beaten. See [Sicily & America](../analysis/sicily-and-america.md).

> **"I'm gonna make him an offer he can't refuse."**
> — Vito, to Johnny Fontane. [Watch it](../media/scenes.md#the-offer). Note that Brando throws it away.

> **"Leave the gun. Take the cannoli."**
> — [Clemenza](../characters/clemenza-and-tessio.md), after Paulie Gatto's murder. **Improvised by Richard S. Castellano on set.** Six words that tell you everything about the man.

> **"Luca Brasi sleeps with the fishes."**
> — Clemenza, translating a fish wrapped in a bulletproof vest.

> **"It's not personal, Sonny. It's strictly business."**
> — Michael, proposing to murder two men who shot his father and broke his jaw. Nobody in these films is telling the truth when they say this. See [Themes](../analysis/themes.md).

> **"A man who doesn't spend time with his family can never be a real man."**
> — Vito, in the same conversation where he slaps his godson for weeping.

> **"Never tell anybody outside the family what you're thinking again."**
> — Vito to [Sonny](../characters/sonny-corleone.md), after Sonny's flicker of interest in Sollozzo's deal. The single most consequential sentence in the film, and it's a rebuke nobody heeds.

> **"Look how they massacred my boy."**
> — Vito to Bonasera, over Sonny's body — calling in the favor from the opening scene.

> **"I never wanted this for you… I work my whole life, I don't apologize, to take care of my family."**
> — Vito to Michael, the garden scene. **Written by Robert Towne, uncredited, and not in the novel.** See [Book vs. Film](../analysis/book-vs-film.md).

> **"Do you renounce Satan?" — "I do renounce him."**
> — The priest and Michael, during [five murders](../analysis/baptism-sequence.md).

> **"Don't ask me about my business, Kay."**
> — Michael. Said twice, in two films, and it is always a door closing.

> **"Tell Mike it was only business. I always liked him."**
> — [Tessio](../characters/clemenza-and-tessio.md), being led away to be killed.

> **"I made my bones when you were going out with cheerleaders."**
> — [Moe Greene](../characters/moe-greene.md), sealing it.

## Part II (1974)

> **"Keep your friends close, but your enemies closer."**
> — Michael, attributing it to his father. **Vito never says it in any film.** See below.

> **"I know it was you, Fredo. You broke my heart."**
> — Michael to [Fredo](../characters/fredo-corleone.md), Havana. A death sentence and a genuine grief in one sentence.

> **"I'm smart! Not like everybody says… I'm smart and I want respect!"**
> — Fredo, the trilogy's only howl.

> **"This is the business we've chosen."**
> — [Hyman Roth](../characters/hyman-roth.md). Followed by *I didn't ask who gave the order — because it had nothing to do with business*, which is a threat, an accusation, and an alibi at once.

> **"There are many things my father taught me here in this room. He taught me: keep your friends close, but your enemies closer."**
> — the full version.

> **"If anything in this life is certain — if history has taught us anything — it's that you can kill anyone."**
> — Michael, watching a Cuban rebel detonate a grenade rather than be captured.

> **"Michael, we're bigger than U.S. Steel."**
> — Hyman Roth, on the Havana syndicate.

> **"It wasn't a miscarriage. It was an abortion."**
> — [Kay](../characters/kay-adams.md). The only successful act of resistance anyone mounts against Michael. See [The Women](../analysis/the-women.md).

> **"A man can never lose his family."** — Carmela. **"Times are changing."** — Michael, who has already decided to kill her son.

## Part III (1990) and Coda

> **"Just when I thought I was out… they pull me back in."**
> — Michael. The most-quoted line in the film and the most parodied line in the trilogy.

> **"I killed my mother's son. I killed my father's son."**
> — Michael's confession to Cardinal Lamberto. The only fully true sentence he says in three films. See [Catholicism & Ritual](../analysis/catholicism-and-ritual.md).

> **"Your sins are terrible, and it is just that you suffer. Your life could be redeemed, but I know you don't believe that. You will not change."**
> — Cardinal Lamberto, absolving him.

> **"The richest man is the one with the most powerful friends."**
> — Don Altobello.

> **"Power wears out those who don't have it."**
> — Calò, before killing Don Lucchesi. **The line is Giulio Andreotti's**, the Italian prime minister Lucchesi is modeled on. See [Real-World Mafia](../reference/real-world-mafia.md).

> **"When the Sicilians wish you 'cent'anni,' it means 'for long life'… and a Sicilian never forgets."**
> — the closing title card of [*Coda*](../films/the-godfather-coda-2020.md). A blessing and a threat in one phrase.

## Commonly misquoted

**"Keep your friends close, but your enemies closer."** Michael says this, in *Part II*, attributing it to Vito. **Vito never says it on screen in any film.** It is universally misattributed to Brando's character and often to Sun Tzu, who did not write it either — the nearest classical source is Machiavelli, and even that's a stretch.

**"Leave the gun, take the cannoli."** Correct, and worth knowing it was **improvised**. It is not in the script and not in the novel.

**"Say hello to my little friend."** Not this film. *Scarface* (1983), also Pacino.

**"Behind every great fortune there is a crime."** [Puzo's epigraph](../books/index.md#on-that-balzac-epigraph), attributed to Balzac. Balzac's actual line, from *Le Père Goriot*, is *the secret of great fortunes without apparent cause is a forgotten crime*. The punchier version predates Puzo; *The Godfather* is why everyone knows it.

**"I'm gonna make him an offer he can't refuse."** Frequently rendered as *"I'll make him an offer…"* Both forms appear across the trilogy — Vito says *I'm gonna* in the wedding scene; the AFI's ranked version is that one.
