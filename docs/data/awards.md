# Awards

Twenty-eight competitive Academy Award nominations across three films, nine wins, one declined and one rescinded.

---

## Academy Awards

### The Godfather — 45th Academy Awards (March 27, 1973)

**3 wins from 10 nominations**

| Category | Nominee | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Best Picture** | Albert S. Ruddy | **Won** |
| **Best Actor** | Marlon Brando | **Won — declined** |
| **Best Adapted Screenplay** | Mario Puzo, Francis Ford Coppola | **Won** |
| Best Director | Francis Ford Coppola | Lost — Bob Fosse, *Cabaret* |
| Best Supporting Actor | Al Pacino | Lost — Joel Grey, *Cabaret* |
| Best Supporting Actor | James Caan | Lost |
| Best Supporting Actor | Robert Duvall | Lost |
| Best Costume Design | Anna Hill Johnstone | Lost |
| Best Film Editing | William Reynolds, Peter Zinner | Lost |
| Best Sound | Bud Grenzbach, Richard Portman, Christopher Newman | Lost |

**Notes.** [Pacino](../people/al-pacino.md) is on screen longer than [Brando](../people/marlon-brando.md) and was nominated in Supporting; he objected publicly and reportedly boycotted the ceremony. Three of the film's own actors split the supporting category. **[Gordon Willis](../people/gordon-willis.md) was not nominated.** [Nino Rota's](../people/nino-rota.md) score nomination was **rescinded** — see below.

### The Godfather Part II — 47th Academy Awards (April 8, 1975)

**6 wins from 11 nominations**

| Category | Nominee | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Best Picture** | Coppola, Gray Frederickson, Fred Roos | **Won** |
| **Best Director** | Francis Ford Coppola | **Won** |
| **Best Supporting Actor** | Robert De Niro | **Won** |
| **Best Adapted Screenplay** | Coppola, Puzo | **Won** |
| **Best Original Dramatic Score** | Nino Rota, Carmine Coppola | **Won** |
| **Best Art Direction** | Dean Tavoularis, Angelo Graham, George R. Nelson | **Won** |
| Best Actor | Al Pacino | Lost — Art Carney, *Harry and Tonto* |
| Best Supporting Actor | Michael V. Gazzo | Lost |
| Best Supporting Actor | Lee Strasberg | Lost |
| Best Supporting Actress | Talia Shire | Lost |
| Best Costume Design | Theadora Van Runkle | Lost |

**Notes.** The **first sequel ever to win Best Picture**, matched since only by *The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King*. *The Godfather* and *Part II* are the **only original/sequel pair to both win**. Gazzo and Strasberg split the supporting vote against their castmate De Niro. Pacino's loss to Art Carney is routinely listed among the Academy's worst decisions.

## The two footnotes

### Brando's refusal

He won Best Actor and did not attend. **Sacheen Littlefeather** declined it on his behalf, citing the industry's treatment of Native Americans and the standoff at Wounded Knee. She was given about sixty seconds and improvised; Brando's prepared statement ran to pages and she read it publicly afterward.

The Academy **formally apologized to her in 2022**, shortly before her death. Her claimed Apache heritage was disputed by her own family after she died, and the matter remains unresolved.

### Rota's rescinded nomination

[Nino Rota's](../people/nino-rota.md) score for *The Godfather* was nominated and then **withdrawn** when it emerged the Love Theme was adapted from his own 1958 score for *Fortunella*. The rule barred previously used material. The AFI later ranked the same score **#5 among all film scores**.

He won two years later for *Part II*.

## BAFTA

**The Godfather:** wins for Brando (Best Actor) and Nino Rota (Anthony Asquith Award for Film Music); nominations including Pacino as Most Promising Newcomer.

**Part II:** Al Pacino won Best Actor (shared recognition with *Dog Day Afternoon*); De Niro nominated as Most Promising Newcomer; Rota nominated for music; the editors nominated.
