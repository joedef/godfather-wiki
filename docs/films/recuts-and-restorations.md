# Recuts & Restorations

There are more versions of *The Godfather* than of almost any comparable American film: four theatrical cuts, three chronological reassemblies, two major restorations, and a long tail of home video transfers of wildly varying quality. This page catalogues them and tells you which to watch.

---

## The chronological reassemblies

All three take existing footage and reorder it into straight chronological sequence, restoring deleted material. All three are interesting. **None of them should be your first exposure to the films** — see [Start Here](../start-here.md#which-version-do-i-watch) for why.

### The Godfather Saga (1977)

Broadcast on NBC over four nights beginning **November 18, 1977**. Coppola supervised.

Both films, cut apart and reassembled in chronological order from 1901 to 1959, with roughly **an hour of previously unseen footage** restored — and, because this was network television in 1977, with the violence and profanity substantially reduced. The running time was about 7 hours 14 minutes including commercial breaks.

*The Saga* is the origin of nearly every "restored scene" later fans know: Vito's Prohibition operations, Michael in Sicily after Apollonia's death, Genco Abbandando's deathbed, more of Sonny, more of Clemenza. It also established the format every later reassembly copied.

### The Godfather Epic / The Complete Novel for Television (1981)

The VHS box-set release of essentially the *Saga* material, this time **uncensored** — the restored footage plus the original violence. Long out of print and prized by collectors, largely superseded by the 1992 version.

### The Godfather Trilogy: 1901–1980 (1992)

Coppola's own update, released on VHS and laserdisc after *Part III* existed. All three films chronologically interleaved, with further restored material, running roughly **583 minutes (9 hours 43 minutes)**.

This is the most complete chronological version, and its structure exposes something the theatrical cuts hide: laid end to end, the Corleone story is a straight decline with one long false dawn in the middle. It is also, as an experience, exhausting and considerably less powerful than the films it's made of.

### The Godfather Trilogy (2020, streaming era)

Various streaming services have offered a chronological playlist option. This is a playback convenience, not an authored cut; the individual films are unmodified.

## The restorations

### The Coppola Restoration (2008)

By the mid-2000s the original camera negative of *The Godfather* was in genuine trouble. The 1972 release had run so long, with so many prints struck, that the negative was physically worn; the protection dupe negative had been lost in Paramount's archive.

In 2006 Coppola approached **Steven Spielberg** — DreamWorks had just been acquired by Paramount — and a restoration was authorized. **Robert A. Harris** of The Film Preserve, the industry's foremost restorer, led it, with [Gordon Willis](../people/gordon-willis.md) himself supervising the color timing. Physical repair of the negatives began in **November 2006**; 4K scanning and digital restoration followed; the work took about eighteen months.

Released **September 23, 2008** on DVD and Blu-ray. Dave Kehr in *The New York Times* wrote that it recovered the golden glow of the original theatrical screenings. It is the version that finally put Willis's actual photography in front of home audiences — every previous video transfer had been brightened, sometimes drastically, by technicians who believed the darkness was an error.

### The 50th Anniversary restoration (2022)

For the film's fiftieth, Paramount restored and remastered all three films plus *Coda* at 4K with HDR, from the 2008 work forward. A limited theatrical run began **March 22, 2022** (IMAX and premium formats), followed by 4K UHD Blu-ray and streaming.

This is the current reference version. Differences from 2008 are subtle and mostly about highlight handling and grain retention rather than any reinterpretation.

## Which version to watch

| If you want… | Watch |
| --- | --- |
| The films as intended, best quality | **2022 4K remasters** of the theatrical cuts, in release order |
| The best available *Part III* | **[Coda](the-godfather-coda-2020.md)** (2020) |
| Everything that was ever shot | *The Godfather Trilogy: 1901–1980* (1992), then the deleted-scene supplements on the 2008/2022 discs |
| The historical curiosity | *The Godfather Saga* (1977) — for the television edit itself, not the story |
| To avoid | Any pre-2008 home video transfer. The brightening is severe and it destroys the photography. |

## A note on runtimes

Published runtimes for these films disagree constantly. Causes: PAL speed-up on European releases (a 4% shortening), the 1991 *Part III* home video cut running ~170 minutes against the 162-minute theatrical, whether the studio logo and end credits are counted, and simple repetition of wrong numbers.

Figures used on this wiki are the US theatrical runtimes: **175 / 202 / 162 / 158**. *Part II* in particular is variously cited at 200, 202, and 205 minutes; 202 is the most commonly attested.
