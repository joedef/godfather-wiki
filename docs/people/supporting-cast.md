# Supporting Cast

The character actors who make the trilogy feel like a world rather than a story.

---

## The Godfather (1972)

**Richard S. Castellano** — [Peter Clemenza](../characters/clemenza-and-tessio.md). A Bronx-born stage actor who had been nominated for an Oscar the previous year for *Lovers and Other Strangers*. **The cannoli line is his**, contributed on set. He did not return for *Part II*; the most persistent account is a dispute over money and his wish to write his own dialogue, and the character was killed off-screen and replaced by [Frank Pentangeli](../characters/frank-pentangeli.md). He later starred in the sitcom *The Super* and died in 1988, aged 55.

**Abe Vigoda** — [Salvatore Tessio](../characters/clemenza-and-tessio.md). A 51-year-old stage actor, essentially unknown, cast for the face. He became far more famous as Detective Fish on *Barney Miller* and *Fish*, and then — after a 1982 magazine erroneously reported his death — spent thirty-four years as the subject of a running national joke about whether he was still alive, which he participated in with total good humor. He was, until January 26, 2016, aged 94.

**Sterling Hayden** — [Capt. McCluskey](../characters/sollozzo-and-mccluskey.md). The most extraordinary biography in the cast: merchant seaman, OSS operative who ran guns to Yugoslav partisans in WWII, briefly a Communist Party member, and a HUAC witness in 1951 who named names and spent the rest of his life calling it the shame of his existence. Also *The Asphalt Jungle*, *The Killing*, and General Ripper in *Dr. Strangelove*. Died 1986.

**Al Lettieri** — [Virgil Sollozzo](../characters/sollozzo-and-mccluskey.md). A superb heavy — *The Getaway*, *Mr. Majestyk* — reportedly connected to organized crime by marriage, which was not unusual on this production. Died of a heart attack in 1975, aged 47.

**Richard Conte** — [Emilio Barzini](../characters/barzini-and-the-five-families.md). A *film noir* veteran (*Cry of the City*, *The Big Combo*, *Thieves' Highway*) who had himself been considered for Vito. Plays the film's actual antagonist in about a dozen lines. Died 1975.

**Alex Rocco** — [Moe Greene](../characters/moe-greene.md). Had a genuine background in the Boston underworld during the Irish Gang War before moving west. Later won an Emmy for *The Famous Teddy Z*. Died 2015.

**John Marley** — Jack Woltz. A veteran character actor, Oscar-nominated for *Love Story*. The scream is his. Died 1984.

**Lenny Montana** — [Luca Brasi](../characters/luca-brasi.md). A professional wrestler and genuine Colombo family associate, not an actor, whose real nervousness rehearsing his speech to the Don was kept and built into a scene. Died 1992.

**Al Martino** — [Johnny Fontane](../characters/johnny-fontane.md). A real star — "Here in My Heart" was the first UK number one in 1952 — with real mob connections; his godfather was the Pennsylvania boss Russell Bufalino. Cast, dropped when Coppola arrived, and reinstated under pressure nobody has ever fully explained. Died 2009.

**Morgana King** — [Carmela Corleone](../characters/carmela-corleone.md). A jazz contralto with a substantial recording career and essentially no acting background. She is singing at the wedding, undubbed. Died 2018.

**Gianni Russo** — [Carlo Rizzi](../characters/carlo-rizzi.md). Not an actor; talked his way in and screen-tested by choreographing the street fight. Has told a great many stories about his own background since, most of them undocumented.

**Simonetta Stefanelli** — [Apollonia](../characters/apollonia-vitelli.md). Sixteen at the time of filming; a substantial subsequent career in Italian cinema. Dubbed in the English release.

**Richard Bright** — Al Neri, in all three films. A New York stage and screen fixture. Killed by a bus in 2006.

**John Cazale**, **Talia Shire**, and the principals have their own pages: see [People](index.md).

## Part II (1974)

**Lee Strasberg** — [Hyman Roth](../characters/hyman-roth.md). Artistic director of the Actors Studio and the most influential acting teacher in America; [Pacino's](al-pacino.md) own teacher. He was 72 and had barely acted in decades. Nominated for Best Supporting Actor. Died 1982.

**Michael V. Gazzo** — [Frank Pentangeli](../characters/frank-pentangeli.md). Primarily a **playwright** — *A Hatful of Rain* (1955) was a landmark Broadway drama — who acted rarely. Nominated for Best Supporting Actor. Died 1995.

**Bruno Kirby** — young [Clemenza](../characters/clemenza-and-tessio.md). Later *This Is Spinal Tap*, *When Harry Met Sally…*, *City Slickers*. Died 2006, aged 57.

**Gastone Moschin** — Don Fanucci. A major Italian stage and screen actor, best known at home for the *Amici Miei* films. Died 2017.

**Dominic Chianese** — Johnny Ola. Went on to play Uncle Junior on *The Sopranos*, which makes his appearance here retroactively load-bearing for the whole genre.

**Danny Aiello** — Tony Rosato. Improvised the line *Michael Corleone says hello* while garroting Pentangeli — which is the reason Pentangeli spends the rest of the film believing Michael tried to kill him. Later Oscar-nominated for *Do the Right Thing*. Died 2019.

**G. D. Spradlin** — Senator Pat Geary. A former oil lawyer who became American cinema's definitive corrupt official. Died 2011.

**Oreste Baldini** — Vito Andolini, aged nine. Carries the film's opening ten minutes almost wordlessly.

**Roger Corman** — a senator. Coppola's first employer, repaying the favor. **Harry Dean Stanton** plays an FBI agent. **Troy Donahue** plays Connie's fiancé "Merle Johnson," which was Donahue's real name.

## Part III (1990)

**Eli Wallach** — Don Altobello. *The Magnificent Seven*, *The Good, the Bad and the Ugly*, a founding member of the Actors Studio, and — at 75 — the most purely enjoyable thing in the film. He got the part after Frank Sinatra agreed to it and couldn't clear the schedule. Died 2014, aged 98.

**Joe Mantegna** — [Joey Zasa](../characters/zasa-altobello-lucchesi.md). A Mamet regular (*Glengarry Glen Ross* on Broadway, *House of Games*), and one of the few *Part III* performances nobody complained about.

**Raf Vallone** — Cardinal Lamberto. A distinguished Italian actor and former journalist and footballer. Gives the film its best scene, opposite Pacino in the confession. Died 2002.

**Bridget Fonda** — Grace Hamilton. **George Hamilton** — B. J. Harrison, the character invented to fill [Tom Hagen](../characters/tom-hagen.md)'s absence. **Franco Citti** returns as Calò, seventeen years on. **Helmut Berger** — Keinszig; a Visconti veteran.

**Franc D'Ambrosio** — Anthony Corleone. An actual opera singer, which is why the Teatro Massimo sequence works.
