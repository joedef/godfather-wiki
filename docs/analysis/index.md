## A note on method

Two rules govern everything in this section.

**Interpretation is labeled.** Where a reading is contested, the page says who contests it. Where Coppola has stated an intention, that's given as his stated intention rather than as the meaning — a director's account of his own film is evidence, not verdict.

**The films get to disagree with themselves.** *The Godfather* is not a coherent moral argument and doesn't try to be. It is seductive about the thing it condemns; it gives its most repellent characters its best manners; it makes you enjoy a garroting. Any reading that resolves all of that has flattened it.

Corrections and counter-readings are the most welcome contribution here. See [Contributing](https://github.com/joedef/godfather-wiki/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md).
