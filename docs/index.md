# The Godfather Wiki

<p class="epigraph">I believe in America. America has made my fortune.
<cite>Amerigo Bonasera — the first words of the film</cite></p>

An open, community-maintained reference to **The Godfather** — Mario Puzo's 1969 novel, Francis Ford Coppola's films, the continuations that followed, and the people who made all of it.

Two things live here side by side. The first is a **reference work**: who's who, what happens, what it cost, what it won, where it was shot, which version you're watching. The second is an **analysis**: what the trilogy is actually doing with light, with time, with the sacraments, and with a man who believed he was different from his father.

If you want the short version of why any of this is worth a wiki: *The Godfather* is a film about a decent man who becomes a monster, told so patiently that most viewers don't notice the moment it happens. Finding that moment is the whole project.

*Part II* is the first sequel ever to win Best Picture, and one of only two in the Academy's history. *Part I* and *Part II* are the only original-and-sequel pair to both win. All three films were nominated — a feat matched only by *The Lord of the Rings*.

## The books

Puzo wrote a potboiler to pay off gambling debts and accidentally wrote the defining American novel about immigration and business. Then other people kept writing them.

- [**The Godfather**](books/the-godfather-1969.md) (1969) — Puzo. 67 weeks on the bestseller list; over nine million copies in two years.
- [**The Sicilian**](books/the-sicilian-1984.md) (1984) — Puzo. Michael's exile, wrapped around the true story of the bandit Salvatore Giuliano.
- [**The Godfather Returns**](books/the-godfather-returns-2004.md) (2004) and [**The Godfather's Revenge**](books/the-godfathers-revenge-2006.md) (2006) — Mark Winegardner, chosen by Random House from thousands of entrants.
- [**The Family Corleone**](books/the-family-corleone-2012.md) (2012) — Ed Falco, built from an unproduced Puzo screenplay, and the subject of a lawsuit.
